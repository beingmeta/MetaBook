Navigating your sBook

The overleaf at the top of the page shows a map of your book centered on the current location. You can use it to browse or navigate the book.

Tapping Clicking on the map jumps to the corresponding location in the book;

Pressing or holding lets you glimpse the location without actually jumping there.

Layout stripes display the arrangement and sizes of divisions at a particular level in the book. The segment for the current section is light blue and contains a red tick indicates your relative position.

Subsection clouds appear when you're at the beginning of a section which has its own subsections. It consists of a layout stripe for all of its subsections together a cloud of subsection names. Clicking a name or segment jumps to that subsection.

Type your comments, ending with Enter or tapping clicking the right-most icon.

Add links as [@uri title], e.g. [@http://www.whitehouse.gov/ The White House].

Add tags by typing in the tags field, enclosing note text in [brackets] or tappingclicking suggested tags in the cloud.

Specify synonyms for your tags (e.g. [airplane|plane|aircraft]) or describe relations (e.g. [sloop|^sailboat|^boat]) to make your tags more useful for browsing and search by yourself or others.

Skimming by Sections

The overleaf at the top of the page shows the current section with the arrangement and sizes of the surrounding sections in the book.

Clicking Tapping on a segment jumps to the beginning of that section. Holding the mouse button down overPressing on a segment shows you a preview of the section without going there.

Tap Click the buttons to move forward or back by sections at the current level. Shift-clicking (holding the shift key while clicking) in the right or left margin moves forward or backward by section at the current level. Swiping with two fingers advances by sections at the current level, as does double-tapping the left or right edge.

Scanning Glosses or Results

You're now scanning through parts of your book based on either glosses or search results. Tap Click on the content to stop scanning.

The overleaf at the top of the page shows a summary of the current gloss or search result, including links, tags, excerpt, etc. Its location in the book is indicated in the grey bar at the top of the summary. Tap Click the gray bar to return to the list of glosses or results. Tap elsewhere to expand the summary (or expand text or follow links).

Swiping with two fingers (right or left) moves to the previous/next result or gloss.

The left and right arrow keys move to the previous/next result or gloss.

The double arrows also move to the previous/next gloss or search result, as does tapping with two fingers shift-clicking in the left or right margin.

The top line of the overleaf shows the location of the result in the book's structure, followed by either the gloss itself (with its text, tags, links, and excerpts) or the content tags assigned to the passage and used for search.

Enter a page number at the top of the page to jump directly to that page.
Use the escape key or tap click at the bottom of the page to cancel.

Enter a numeric percentage (in the field at the top of the page) to jump directly to that point within the book.
These percentages will remain stable even as page layout or font size changes. Use the escape key or tap click at the bottom of the page to cancel and return to the text.

Searching your sBook

You are searching for passages or glosses within your book, using either tags or literals. As you type or edit in the search box, the cloud of tags and phrases will grow or shrink to display matching phrases, tags, or synonyms of tags.

Tapping Clicking adds a phrase or tag to the current search, generating both results and an updated cloud of phrases and tags. Tags may be related to other tags (for example, dachshund to dog) and those relationships are used in your search.

Once you have specified at least one tag or phrase, you can see the matching results by either tapping clicking on the result count or using the Enter key in an empty input box. If there are few enough results, it will switch to them automatically.

Browsing search results

These are the results of your search, ordered by relevance and position within the book. You can return to the tag/phrase cloud by tapping clicking in the text box or on the “DDD associated tags” beneath it.

Tap Click a result to jump to that part of the book and begin scanning the book based on the results. When scanning, you can use the double-arrow buttons on the edges to move through the results (you can also use two-fingered swipes your keyboard's arrow keys).

Holding the mouse button down onPressing and holding a result shows you a glimpse of the result's context without leaving the list of results.

Browsing all glosses

These are all of the glosses overlaid on your copy of this book.

The pictures or icons at the top of the page show the creators or overlays for all of the glosses; tap click to limit the list to glosses added by a particular person or shared thorough a particular overlay. Using the Shift key adds that individual or overlay to the displayed list. Tap Click again to clear your selection.

Tap Click a gloss to jump to the location where it has been applied; you can then begin scanning the book based on the selected glosses. When scanning, you can use the double-arrow buttons on the edges to move through the glosses (you can also use two-fingered swipes your keyboard's arrow keys).

Holding the mouse button down onPressing and holding a gloss will show you a glimpse of the gloss's context without actually leaving the list of glosses.
