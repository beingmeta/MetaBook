Navigating your sBook

The overleaf at the top of the page is a map of your book centered on the current location. You can use it to browse or navigate the book.

Layout stripes display the arrangement and sizes of divisions at a particular level in the book. The segment for the current section is light blue and contains a red tick indicates your relative position. Tapping Clicking a segment jumps to where it starts.

Subsection maps appear when you're at the beginning of a section with its own subsections. It consists of a layout stripe for all of its subsections together a cloud of subsection names. Clicking a name or segment jumps to that subsection.

Skimming by Sections

The overleaf at the top of the page shows the current section with the arrangement and sizes of surrounding sections.

Moving the mouse over a segment in the map shows information about the section in a tooltip. Clicking Tapping on a segment jumps to the beginning of that section.

Tap Click the buttons to move forward or back by sections at the current level. Shift-clicking in the right or left margin moves forward or backward by section at the current level. Swiping with two fingers advances by sections at the current level, as does double-tapping the left or right edge.

Scanning Glosses or Results

You're now skimming through a set of slices from this book based on either overlaid glosses or the results of a search.

Tapping Clicking on the double arrows moves to the previous/next gloss or search result, as does tapping with two fingers shift-clicking in the left or right margin.

Swiping with two fingers (right or left) also moves among the results.

The left and right arrow keys also move among the results.

The overleaf at the top of the page shows the current gloss or search result. The top line shows the location of the result in the book's structure, followed by either the gloss itself (with its text, tags, links, and excerpts) or the content tags assigned to the passage and used for search.
