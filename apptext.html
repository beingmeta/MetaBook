This sBooks translucent HUD (heads up display) provides rich context and interaction around this book's content, displaying structure, tags, and glosses from sources you've selected. The sBooks display HUD is available anywhere but is normally invisible or ghostly gray. Holding down the shift key makes the displays more visible. The display HUD is divided into four core functions:

  • the TOC HUD is used to navigate the book's structure
  • the Feed HUD, a bar on the left of the page, to browse glosses added to the book by friends, colleagues, or subscriptions;
  • the Search HUD is used to recall or discover content based on semantic tags provided by the publisher or added glosses, and
  • individual gloss marks in the right hand margin indicate glosses added by friends, colleagues, or subscribed “metadocs”

In addition, the application HUD (which you're probably using right now) enables you to get help, configure sBooks, identify yourself, adjust the interface, and engage socially by inviting friends to share comments, joining or starting reading circles, publishing or subscribing to metadocs, and more. You can activate the application HUD by tapping/clicking on the sBooks icon on the left of the page.

Reading. Read the document as you would normally, using browser scrolling (bars or keys), space/backspace, or the translucent arrow buttons at the bottom of the page. As you move across sections or passages, parts of the HUD will briefly flash when their content (position in the table of contents, tags associated with a passage) changes. Where icons appear in the right margin, they indicate a gloss (comments, tags, references) added by a friend, associate, or subscription. Clicking on the icon displays the indicated glosses and allows you to add your own.

You can also add your own gloss by simply tapping/clicking twice on the text (once to target, again to activate). A gloss typically includes a short note (“What do you think”) together with tags, extended comments, or external references. You can also specify who can see your gloss: your friends, reading circles you're a part of, subscribers to metadocs you are publishing, or just yourself.

Navigating. Type 'N' or touch the compass in the upper left corner to bring the Navigation HUD at the top of the screen. The Navigation HUD is a dynamic "Table of Contents" consisting of a series of stripes reflecting levels in the document. Each stripe bears the name of a section and a segmented bar indicating its subsections. Red ticks indicate the current location among the subsections and the background of each title indicates your position in the named section.

As you move your pointer over elements of the TOC, the corresponding part of the document will be previewed; moving the pointer away previews different content or returns to the current location; tapping jumps to the previewed content. The preview function allows you to 'flip through' the book by simply moving the pointer over the displayed contents .

Glosses are comments, tags, or references associated with sections of the text. Glosses are organized into feeds (users, groups, metadocs) and listed (as icons) in the vertical bar on the left hand side of the screen. Clicking an icon shows the glosses from that feed. Clicking the 'everyone' icon at the bottom displays all glosses in the order of the document.

Glosses are organized by where they occur and clicking on a gloss jumps to that location where the gloss was added; pressing the control key or holding the mouse button temporarily previews that location.

Searching. Type 'F' or click the icon in the upper right corner to bring up the search HUD (again at the top of the screen). Search in sBooks uses semantic tags which are based on interconnected 'concepts' rather than keywords. This means, for example, that a search on 'dog' can find a passage tagged with 'dachshund' (and not 'hot dog'). Tags can be provided by either the document itself (in the HTML markup) or by glosses merged into the document from your friends, reading circles, or metadocs. The semantic knowledge &emdash; for example, that dachshunds are dogs &emdash; can be provided as part of the tagging or through external knowlets.

As you type in the search HUD, matching tags are automatically displayed: clicking on a tag selects it. Tags are separated by semicolons Enter) brings you to the search results; clicking the text box brings you back to the search cloud.

The results of the search are based on both tags from the publisher and tags from any merged glosses. The glosses appear with a picture of the origin (feed) for the gloss and the short text and tags it assigns; icons will display more extended text () or cross references () when available. As with glosses, clicking on a search result goes to the location; holding down the control key or mouse button previews its content.
