Navigating your sBook

The outline overleaf at the top of the page shows a partial map of your book centered on the current location. You can use it to browse or navigate the book. The blue regions indicate your current location.

Tapping Clicking on the map jumps to the corresponding location in the book;

Pressing or holding lets you glimpse the location without actually jumping there.

Layout stripes display the arrangement and sizes of divisions at a particular level in the book. The segment for the current section is light blue and contains a red bar indicating your relative position.

Subsection clouds appear when you're at the beginning of a section which has its own subsections. It consists of a layout stripe for all of its subsections together with a cloud of subsection names. TappingClicking a name or segment jumps to that subsection. PressingClicking and holding a name shows the corresponding segment; TappingClicking the segment lets you glimpse the start of that section.

Skimming by Sections

The overleaf at the top of the page shows the current section with the arrangement and sizes of its surrounding sections.

Clicking Tapping on a segment jumps to the beginning of that section. Holding the mouse button down overPressing on a segment shows you a preview of the section without going there. Releasing jumps to the section and tapping the tablet edgestapping a key brings you back.

Swiping with two fingers advances by sections at the current level, as does double-tapping the left or right edge.

Scanning Glosses or Results

You're now scanning through your book based on a list of glosses or search results. The current gloss or search result is shown in the overleaf at the top of the page.

Tap Click on the overleaf to expand it and and on the content to stop scanning. Press and hold the overleaf to return to the list of results or glosses.

The double arrows and swiping horizontally (right or left) the left and right arrow keys move forward or back through the glosses or search results.

The top line of the overleaf shows the location of the result in the book overall, both in the outline (the text links) and the in content as a whole (the blue span). Tapping Clicking on it also returns to the results/gloss list.

Enter a page number at the top of the page to jump directly to that page.
Use the escape key or tap click at the bottom of the page to cancel.

Enter a numeric percentage (in the field at the top of the page) to jump directly to that point within the book.
These percentages will remain stable even as page layout or font size changes. Use the escape key or tap click at the bottom of the page to cancel and return to the text.

Searching your sBook

You are searching for passages or glosses within your book, using either tags or literals. As you type or edit in the search box, the cloud of tags and phrases will grow or shrink to display matching phrases, tags, or synonyms of tags.

Tapping Clicking adds a phrase or tag to the current search, generating both results and an updated cloud of phrases and tags. Tags may be related to other tags (for example, dachshund to dog) and those relationships are used in your search.

Once you have specified at least one tag or phrase, you can see the matching results by either tapping clicking on the result count or using the Enter key in an empty input box. If there are few enough results, it will switch to them automatically.

Browsing search results

These are the results of your search, ordered by relevance and position within the book. You can return to the tag/phrase cloud by tapping clicking in the text box or on the “DDD associated tags” beneath it.

Tap Click a result to jump to that part of the book and begin scanning the book based on the results. When scanning, you can use the double-arrow buttons on the edges to move through the results (you can also use two-fingered swipes your keyboard's arrow keys).

Holding the mouse button down onPressing and holding a result shows you a glimpse of the result's context without leaving the list of results.

Browsing all glosses

These are all of the glosses applied to your copy of this book, displayed as a series of cards in the center of the browser page.

The pictures or icons at the top of the browser page show the creators or layers where they originate; tap click an icon to see only the glosses added by a particular person or shared thorough a particular layer. Using the Shift key adds that individual or layer to the displayed list. Tap Click again to clear your selection.

Tap Click a card to jump to the location where the gloss is applied; you can scan through the book based on the selected glosses. When scanning, you can use the double-arrow buttons on the edges to move through the glosses (you can also use two-fingered swipes your keyboard's arrow keys).

Holding the mouse button down onPressing and holding a card will show you a glimpse of the gloss's context without actually leaving the list of glosses.
