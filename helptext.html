This sBooks translucent HUD (heads up display) provides context and interaction for the book's content. The sBooks display HUD is available anywhere but is normally invisible or light gray. Holding down the shift key makes the displays more visible. The display HUD is divided into four elements:

  • the TOC HUD on the top for navigation
  • the Meta HUD, on the left, to browse glosses
  • the Index HUD on the bottom to search tags, and
  • individual note icons in the right hand margin.

Preview icons () appear throughout the HUD: moving the mouse over these icons temporarily previews the content, scrolling to the referenced point in the document and dimming the HUD. Clicking goes to the displayed content; moving the mouse away restores the document location and returns to the HUD.

Display HUDs are automatically updated as you read the document: moving the mouse over a HUD makes it more visible and clicking expands the displayed information, making it persistent until you click again.
You can make (and share) notes in your sBook by clicking on a passage or heading. Providing you're logged into sBooks (and Facebook), you can then a brief comment together with tags, longer details, excerpts, or cross references.

The TOC HUD has collapsed and expanded forms and clicking on the HUD or the NAV button, toggles between them. In the collapsed view, you see the name of the current section and a bar of alternating spans indicating the surrounding sections. The lengths of the spans indicate the relative length of the sections and the current section is yellow. Moving the mouse over a span previews the head of the corresponding section, analogous to flipping through the pages of a physical book. Clicking jumps to the previewed section. In the expanded view, all of the sections above the current section are displayed from top to bottom and each works just as the collapsed view.

In the Index HUD, you can search the book using tags assigned to content (automatically or manually) by the content authors, your friends, or any subscribed metadocs. The Index HUD is especially designed to help you search for combinations of tags within the book. In the text field, tags — which can include spaces — are separated by semicolons. Above the text field is a cloud of possible tags and a result count based on the complete tags you've typed so far and the tag you are currently typing. The cloud of tags is ordered by frequency among the current search results. Clicking a tag adds that tag to the search; clicking the results count displays the results. From the keyboard, typing Enter or semicolon picks the most prominent completion or (if there's no additional text) Enter shows the results.
